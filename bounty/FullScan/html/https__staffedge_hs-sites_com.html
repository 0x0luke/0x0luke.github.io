Success Stories

SHEILA MUSGROVE
PRESIDENT, TAG RECRUITMENT GROUP INC.

Tag Leads the Way in the West

In four short years, Tag Recruitment has managed to achieve some great recognition from the business community including:   Emerging Entrepreneur of the Year from the Calgary Chamber of Commerce, Profit Hot 50 (ranked 24th) of Canada’s Emerging Growth Companies as well as being recognized by Alberta Venture Magazine in 2009 as the 10th Fastest Growing Company in Alberta under 20 million.

We asked founder, Sheila Musgrove, what is the key to her success.

Did you ever think you would be where you are today?

Obviously, after engaging new members for the Staffing Edge as a sales rep, it became pretty clear what a great portfolio it represented, but as well it underscored that you didn’t need a few million to start a staffing company.

What do you believe are the key components to that success?

Where we are today, has far exceeded where I thought that we could be in this short amount of time!  We have a great team, a growing volume of success stories from our clients and candidates, a brand and company that continually stand out amongst the massive amount of choice of staffing firms in the city of Calgary and Province.

What advice would you give to an entrepreneur starting in the business?

Roll up your sleeves and be accountable to do every role in the company.  In the early days of opening, it was a company of one, so you learn to run out and sell, come back to the office to source and recruit and then do it all over again.

How has The Staffing Edge helped you with your success?

We didn’t have to stop the sales and recruitment process to figure out how we were going to cover a massive increase in payroll along with on-going costs of carrying a large volume of receivables.  We also had immediate access to an expert safety and risk management team to ensure that any WCB claims/issues were strongly managed.   It is wonderful to not have to worry about having to staff for all of the roles that are filled by the Staffing Edge – finance, safety and risk management, insurance and technology.  Our success is their success, so they are there to lend a hand with assistance for just about everything.  And, yes, even though they are in Ontario, they are there to help us on our time zone!

ELAINE HAY
PRESIDENT, CAMPBELL, EDGAR INC.

Elaine Hay named Chair of the BBB of Mainland BC!

After many years on the Executive Board of Mainland BC's Better Business Bureau, this past May, Elaine was chosen as the Chair of the Bureau for 2009 - 2010.

She opened Campbell, Edgar Inc. in Vancouver in 1993, with a clear vision and a strong determination to improve the Canadian retail experience. Campbell Edgar's specialty is still the retail industry; placing all positions within store operations, head office as well as warehouse, transportation and logistical positions. However, as the years have gone by, the business has expanded into all levels of executive, mid-management and various industries throughout Canada.

"I had been toying with the idea of adding a temporary division as a service to my retail customers, but I must admit, all the time and details of setting up the business and then managing the ongoing administration left me procrastinating" says Elaine. "After meeting with TSE's owner Lou, and chatting with other Staffing Edge members, I was sold on the perfect win-win situation!"

"By being able to provide staff in other ways than just permanent placement, our client's now see us as a 1 stop shop for all their staffing needs. I have come to know and partner with several other TSE members, and I know that we really do care about each others businesses. I know that I can speak with confidence to my clients, that my partners can and will handle my client's temp needs in other parts of the country with the same high level of service that they have come to expect from us - and I believe that the partners who have worked with us know that they can absolutely count on us to make them look good as well!"

Elaine is an active volunteer in several business organizations. She is a long term active member of ACSESS (Elaine is a past president of the BC chapter). In 2006, Elaine was proud to be awarded the TSE Membership Partnership Award and is proud runner up of the 2008 BBB Torch Award for Marketplace Excellence.

PJ FERGUSON
PRESIDENT, ABL EMPLOYMENT

A Candid Talk with a Top Staffing Entrepreneur

When you think back to when you first started your business, did you ever think you would be where you are today? "I have always been aware of the potential of the staffing industry so I think that, even when I began ABL, I pictured it growing to these levels and beyond."

To be one of the top 100 Women Entrepreneurs in Canada is a great achievement. What do you believe are the key components to that success?

"The Top 100 is an individual recognition yet it is based on size of the company. Growing a company to the size that achieves this level of recognition requires going beyond individual effort and developing both efficient processes and a tremendous support network. ABL is fortunate to have a dedicated staff and the systems and processes that have allowed us to grow and prosper."

What advice would you give to an aspiring taffing entrepreneur? "Establish processes, create a committed team and adhere to the ethics and good practices that are key to helping the industry thrive such as maintaining markups, good hiring practices etc. The Staffing Edge is an excellent source of knowledge on these areas. Always focus on sales and service and never get complacent."

What has been your greatest challenge to date? "In retrospect, our greatest challenge was facing the realization that both customers and staff will come and go. ABL must be set up to have variety and dispersal of risk across our business base and our staffing models so that continued prosperity is not based on any one customer or one person (even me)."

How has The Staffing Edge helped you with your success? "My belief is that true growth and prosperity can happen only with effective processes and a support network. The Staffing Edge gives ABL both of those things and more. The processes are efficient and adaptable to my specific needs. Everything from Stafftrak to payroll processes to invoicing processes are seamless parts of my business. WSIB and Credit issues, which are the two highest risks in this business, are proactively managed by The Staffing Edge’s comprehensive approach. As for a support network, not only have the staff been there to help with issues and provide support, being a member company of The Staffing Edge, I have the opportunity to interact with other staffing company owners who share the same challenges as I do."

MANDY GILBERT
CEO, CREATIVE NICHE

Secret to Success

The underlying key to Creative Niche's success is a strong company culture built on trust, collaboration and a committment to industry-leading service at every turn.

At Creative Niche, we're often asked to explain our secret to success. It's simple: from the day CEO, Mandy Gilbert, founded Creative Niche in 2002, her vision was to build a creative staffing and recruitment firm that provided a positive, engaging and humanizing experience for candidates. At the same time, she endeavoured to provide the right creative talent to leading companies quickly, helping them reach their business goals and grow to new heights.

There were changes along the way, of course. When the Great Recession of 2008 - 2009 struck, business was hit hard. Mandy solicited input from her staff, affectionately called 'Nichers', came up with a plan to retain or recoup clients, and managed to carry the company through the tough time with minimal pain.

As Mandy always stresses, the underlying key to Creative Niche's success is a strong company culture built on trust, collaboration and a committment to industry-leading service at every turn. "My advice to other entrepreneurs would be to focus on culture when building their company," she explains. "Our Nichers are the main reason we've enjoyed strong annual revenue growth and attracted major corporate clients." Part of that culture involves giving back to the community, which they spend a lot of time and money doing each year. In fact, Creative Niche donated more than $2 million in time and funding to a wide variety of charities, educational institutions, and professional development initiatives for it's candidate community over the past two years alone.

Key partners such as The Staffing Edge have helped Creative Niche maintain its torrid growth pace and serve their quickly-expanding list of major corporate clients and further grow operations into Europe and the United States. When Creative Niche expanded into the contract workforce management sector in 2009, for example, The Staffing Edge's support and industry expertise helped navigate the numerous operational challenges. "The Staffing Edge has been a key partner for Creative Niche," Gilbert says. "We couldn't have achieved this level of success without them. In addition to providing a number of mission critical services including employer and international payrolling, their consultative approach and valuable insight into employment law, credit, financing and collections has been invaluable. They truly live up to their mission statement by providing the tools to succeed."

ANGELO PITIRRI
OWNER, PRODIGY PERSONNEL

Branch Manager to Business Owner

In September of 2008, after being in the staffing business for four years, Angelo Pitirri went from branch manger to business owner.

I found that I had an incredibly strong passion for the Staffing Industry. I couldn't stop working, the more hours that I worked the happier I was. I found that helping associates find work while helping clients solve their staffing needs was extremely rewarding. Once I started to learn more about The Staffing Edge and the support they could offer to entrepreneurs, I sought out another member of The Staffing Edge who had made a similar move." Angelo explains "the dicussions that I had with him where he shared so many of his positive experiences were very instrumental in building my confidence to make me realize this was the future I wanted".

So what are his fears and challenges? "I am very driven to suceed by the fear of failure, so that was my biggest fear in making that leap. After weighing all the pros and cons, I decided this was a chance worth taking. As an owner I am responsible for so many more things then when I was a manager. Especially being a new business with a new name I have to wear so many more hats. I am the advertising department, the sales force, the after-hours team and on Saturdays the janitor..."

"My original expectations of The Staffing Edge were for back office support for things like payroll, insurance and WSIB but as time has gone on I have learned that The Staffing Edge is an integral part of my day to day operations. They are there for all the big things but more importantly they are always there for the small day to day decisions that I have to make. There is not a situation that comes up that the team at The Staffing Edge has not already experienced so their advice is invaluable. I could not imagine operating this business without them!"
