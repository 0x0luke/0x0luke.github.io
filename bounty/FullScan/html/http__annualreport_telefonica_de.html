Bundesliga Pur – Lunchtime

Early in the second half of the 2018/2019 Bundesliga season, Telefónica Deutschland extended its cooperation with media group SPORT1 MEDIA. Digital mobile after retirement

Telefónica Deutschland supports the digital inclusion of all age groups. The company wants to help everyone reap the benefits of digitalization and enable them to move safely and with self-determination in the digital world. As well as opening up numerous opportunities, Telefónica takes responsibility for explaining the potential risks associated with the internet, enabling people to use it proficiently.

Together with the Digital Opportunities Foundation, Telefónica is engaging with the project “Being digitally mobile in old age” to help get older people more involved in digital technologies and, in doing so, improve their mental and physical mobility. Accompanied by experts, senior citizens will have eight weeks to try out tablet computers and gain experience in using the internet. Telefónica will provide interested older people with a tablet computer and unlimited data free of charge for the duration of the project.

A responsible approach to designing mobile freedom

When Telefónica Deutschland published its Corporate Responsibility Report in 2017, it was the first non-financial report in the company’s history. It promptly received the “Building Public Trust Award 2018” for the best non-financial reporting in the TecDAX category.

LOLA: the emergency app

 

LOLA app is the first digital solution developed for commercial smartphones and smart watches enabling ongoing, everyday health and emergency assistance. It supports users in emergency situations and with their everyday planning – at any time and wherever they are. With this compact and discreet solution, users stay safe wherever they happen to be. 

The development was supported by the Telefónica Deutschland startup program Wayra.

What the LOLA app can do so far:

Automatic fall detection – get help without pressing a button.

 

In an emergency situation, every second counts. LOLA automatically detects falls and will call for help without the user having to press the emergency call button.

Emergency calls using the smart watch – 24/7 safety in case of an emergency.

 

The SOS call is made directly from the smart watch by touching the smart watch display. This function can make all the difference in life-threatening situations by directly connecting to relatives or carers. The app uses GPS to see where the user is and shares their location with the contact person by SMS. In an emergency, not only can the contact get a voice connection with the app user, but they’ll also see exactly where they are, whether they’re in the bathroom, out exercising or in the garden.

Make emergency calls from your smartphone.

 

The SOS alarm can be operated from a smart watch, but also straight from a smartphone. To do so, the installed app doesn’t even need to be opened: the easily accessible notification bar can be used to make an emergency call at any time with ease.

Set contacts individually.

 

You also have the option to select multiple contact people for the smart watch or smartphone to contact in the event of an emergency.

Reminder functions for medicines.

 

LOLA makes it easy for users to manage and check all their medicines from one place. Simply set a medicine schedule on your smartphone or have a family member or carer do so. LOLA will then automatically remind you to take your medicine at the right time. The reminders can be set and saved as one-time or recurring alarms.

Reminder functions for appointments and birthdays.

 

The LOLA smartphone app also lets you save important appointments like visits to the doctor. And it means you’ll never forget another birthday!

Smart City Munich Airport

 

The networking technology at Munich Airport spans over 1,575 hectares – that’s equivalent to 2,200 football pitches. Electricity and gas meters are therefore spread over a huge area which makes it impossible to take readings efficiently. Thanks to Smart Meter Connect from Telefónica Deutschland, manual meter readings by employees are no longer required: The up-to-date reading is automatically submitted every 15 to 180 minutes using a smart camera system.

Network of the future

 

The next step on the road to tomorrow’s mobile communications network is completed. Telefónica study of Internet usage

Germans surf the web just as much on the go as at home: six out of ten consumers no longer distinguish between using the Internet on their mobile or from within their own four walls – they operate in the digital world without compromises, everywhere and at all times; such was the finding of a representative study commissioned by Telefónica Deutschland.

Smart data for public transport – Project xMND 

 

The Extended Mobile Network Data project (xMND) addresses the question of how local public transport can be made sustainable and efficient. Telefónica NEXT and other project partners are collecting data on passenger movement streams in Leipzig and Munich and using the insights from this knowledge to optimise local public transport. The objective is to make local public transport more comfortable and give passengers more precise information and clearer offers.

Source: Leipziger Gruppe

Better air quality with mobile communications data

Reliable methods for analyzing emissions are now entering the focus of public debates about air pollutant values. The solution offered by Telefónica Deutschland: analyzing anonymous mobile communications data. Using this data and taking into account pollutant information from the German Environment Agency on individual transport carriers and weather data has made it more cost-effective to measure Nuremberg’s air quality and eliminated the need for any stationary technology. The project was a success: the new process confirms the existing calculations provided by stationary systems.

Digital Motorway Test Bed

 

Telefónica Deutschland is collaborating with Ericsson and many other partners from politics and industry on networked mobility of the future. That’s why the “Digital Motorway Test Bed” was installed on a 30-kilometre stretch of the A9 motorway in Bavaria between Nuremberg and Greding. One of the services available on the test stretch is a full coverage, high-speed LTE mobile network to help test smart road infrastructure.
